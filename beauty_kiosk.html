Beauty Kiosk

 Interactive Media Design



Project Scope:

  • Learnings: Design Thinking, User Research, Information Architecture, Rapid Prototyping, Usability Testing, Application Development, Testing and Redesign
  • Tools: Sketch
  • Timeline: October 2020 - November 2020
  • Deliverables: Sketches, Low and High fidelity Prototypes, Research findings, Documentation , Report
  • Team: Ashok Kumar Rathod, Ayoola Princewill, Kade Thanuchopond

The Problem

When it comes to adopting an appropriate skincare routine, it is a common-place occurrence to be devoid of the awareness of one’s skin type. As a result, there is an observed general tendency to experiment with various products, to finally arrive at a product that is better suited to one’s skin. As reported in a market survey conducted by Mintel in October 2015, consumers believe that general factors such as regular facial cleansing, lifestyle changes, sunlight exposure, dietary patterns, and adherence to a daily skin regimen impacted their skin’s appearance greater than visiting a dermatologist. Furthermore, with the rise of social media and influencer culture, people find it easier to follow popular opinions and choose products that have garnered a massive following. These behavioral tendencies to experiment with various products, incorporating lifestyle changes aimed at influencing skin and following popular culture strongly suggests that people want to take control of their skincare journey and find comfort in personalizing it for themselves. However, this is a cause for concern for the following reasons. Firstly, experimenting with various products may do greater damage than good. Further, mere lifestyle changes and regular cleansing does not suffice for skincare and there is a strong need for expert interventions. Lastly, adopting products basis influencers’ suggestions may not necessarily suit one’s individual skin-type.

The Solution

We hereby introduce a skin type self-assessment service station, which is supposed to be located in a Cosmetic shop whereby not only customers have an opportunity to identify their skin type, but they also get recommendations on products that would support their skincare endeavors. At the same time, it upholds their preference to personalize their individualistic skincare journeys.

Forming a Hypothesis

Before diving into any problem, I believe it’s important to state a problem hypothesis and then test it. Testing a problem hypothesis allows me to develop a deep understanding of the problem, discover implications, and see if it’s worth solving in the first place.

My Hypothesis

Users feel frustated searching for various skin care products that suit their skin type. Having said that an exclusive app interface that can help you determine your skin type and help find the exact type of products that suit your skin.

User Research

When we developed this hypothesis, we asked ourselves the following:what kind of data do we need to test this hypothesis? Where can we find quick customer feedback data? Where will we find users to validate our ideas?

Reviewing the Reviews

we went to the play store reviews of popular marketplace apps to find out if other users recognized this as a problem.This allowed us to get quick data about users without investing time & energy into recruiting users and interviewing users. We audited over 200 reviews and found that many negative reviews mentioned this problem.

Survey

In order to reach more people and gather more diverse feedback we conducted a survey. The majority of respondents werent happy and unaware about the products they buy online. These are the target users for this app. Respondents were asked to rate the most important factors they consider when buying a beauty product, to help prioritise the features that should be implemented in this interface.

The Opportunity

Based on our research, we feel confident that there is a recognizable amount of frustration in marketplace app users searching for beauty products. If we solve this problem, users will be able to find the right product quickly that matches their skin type without searching through unwanted search results.

Design Goals
BUILD TRUST

Help customers feel confident that they can find the right product they are looking for.

EASE OF USE

The solution should be seamless and easy to use.

Persona Development

For us, it is important to have a persona because it allows us to bring the target users on the journey of designing a solution and addressing the issue. Developing this persona helped us reminisce to how the users feel.

Things to consider

Anyone can come up with an idea and think to themselves "this idea is great, now let's move on and test it." To think out of the box we write down 'Things to consider.' When we break the idea down and question it's value, we often find small issues that need to be addressed. Here are a few of those 'Things to consider:'

  • How to determine a users skin type?
  • How to filter and show products efficiently based on their skin type?
  • What are the important information buyers want to know about the products?
  • How to connect users to answer their needs while maintaining privacy?
  • How to manage the profile for users in the same application?
Sketching & Wireframing - Story board

Early ideas for the beauty station were sketched out repeatedly with pencil and paper in order to form a general idea of the possible screens and user flows. The lightning-fast nature of these paper sketches (often done repeatedly and to a timer) was great for getting ideas out quickly.

User Journey

We generated the user journey to figure out the sequence of interactions that the user is going to perform inside the store. This helped us to quickly evaluate the efficiency of the process needed to achieve a user goal and help pinpoint the execution of the ideas.

LEGEND KEY:

Testing & Iterating

Once we brainstormed different ideas, we wanted to test this idea with users. We initially did some qualitative testing with a few screens to test some variations. And then mocked up screens in Sketch to build our prototype. We conducted usability tests with 6 users.

TEST FINDINGS:
  • Users wanted to have detailed information about the Products.
  • Users prefered to see the number of products available based on the filters applied.
  • Majority of users liked the favourites feature to shortlist products.
USABILITY ISSUE 1:

Users were not enjoying the single large card per row and wanted to see more cards per page.

DESIGN DECISION:

It has been replaced with more cards per row which gave more room to others.

Revisiting the Persona & Problem

This solution will help Julia (the persona) to find the product of her choice quickly and effortlessly. Julia will not have to invest time and energy walking around the streets of Dublin trying to find a cosmetic store without the assurance that she willfind the exact type of product within her budget.


Julia decided to buy a Face Wash. She gets on her phone and opens the app to find the product.She selects the search option and enters all the required filters and chooses a product from the results.

LESSONS LEARNED:
  • Understanding the users and problem deeply at the beginning of a project can go a long way
  • If you have an idea, make sure you can talk about the "why" of the business and address the issue
  • We found it helpful to write down "things to consider" when deciding on a potential solution
