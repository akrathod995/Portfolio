The Problem

The assortment and disposal of solid refuse has been a problem in Sri lanka for many decades. The common modes of disposal include burning and disposing garbage in assortment yards. If this pressing situation is not addressed in a timely manner, these issues are likely to snowball with catastrophic consequences. These such as poor health and quality of life, reduction in life expectancy and further imposes pressure on public health services. It is essential to impart awareness and knowledge about waste disposal at a young age to inculcate behavioural transformation.

The Solution

Sweep City is a mobile application, designed to support the Waste Disposal Awareness Campaign conducted by the Government of Sri Lanka. The application is aimed at sensitising young learners on waste management in a game format that entices them and brings about an attitudinal transition in their approach towards waste management. It also contains an informational repository that engages learners with educational content on waste management.

  • The game is designed around the concept of segregating waste into appropriate trash-cans as that can be easily grasped by young learners.
  • The game will present a series of hurdles to add difficulty to progress towards higher levels
  • Users will receive in-game rewards/points for hitting the right bins. The rewards can be redeemed by users to beautify a virtual area.
  • The app also contains an infotainment repository of engaging content on waste management techniques and upcycling tutorials

User Research

The campaign in Sri Lanka is mainly targeted at young students as the primary target audience. In order to probe deeper into the waste-generating patterns among the student community, primary research was conducted with students based in Limerick.

The objective of the research was to assess the following:

  • Waste generation patterns and habits
  • Extent of current awareness about waste management and recycling techniques.
  • Level of awareness among respondents regarding not-for-profit organizations that mobilize waste.

Findings from Interviews

In addition to the research, interviews were conducted among students to ascertain whether education on waste management and recycling techniques was included in their curriculum. This would indicate whether the schools and institutions regard any importance to these activities in school. Most respondents expressed denial. This further unveiled the need for imparting such awareness at the grassroot level i.e., childhood, the primitive stages of one’s early life.
